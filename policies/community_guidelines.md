# Community Guidelines (Synthetic — Illustrative Only)

> **Note:** This is a SYNTHETIC policy document written for this project to
> demonstrate policy-grounded moderation. It is not the policy of any real
> platform. Its clauses are aligned with the ternary annotation scheme of the
> underlying research (Hateful / Offensive / Clean). Each clause has a stable
> id (`HL-*` hateful, `OF-*` offensive, `CL-*` allowed) and is retrieved
> individually by the adjudicator.

## Protected characteristics

### DEF-1: Protected characteristics
For the purposes of this policy, protected characteristics are: race,
ethnicity, national origin, religion, caste, sexual orientation, gender,
gender identity, disability, and serious disease. Groups defined by one or
more of these characteristics are "protected groups".

## Hateful content (label: Hateful)

### HL-1: Direct attacks on protected groups
Content that attacks, dehumanizes, or expresses contempt for a person or
group of people on the basis of a protected characteristic is Hateful. This
includes comparisons to animals, insects, filth, or disease, and statements
denying a protected group's humanity or right to exist.

### HL-2: Slurs targeting protected groups
Use of slurs — words whose primary function is to demean people for a
protected characteristic — is Hateful when directed at, or used to describe,
members of a protected group. Masked or partially censored slurs (e.g.
"[SLUR]", asterisked variants) are treated the same as the uncensored form
when the target is identifiable.

### HL-3: Incitement and threats against protected groups
Content that threatens violence against a protected group, calls for their
exclusion, expulsion, or harm, or celebrates harm done to them, is Hateful.

### HL-4: Coded and implicit hate
Hateful intent expressed indirectly — through coded language, dog whistles,
stereotypes presented as fact, rhetorical questions, sarcasm, or "just
joking" framing — is Hateful when a reasonable reader would understand the
content as demeaning a protected group. The absence of an explicit slur does
not make such content acceptable.

### HL-5: Hateful comparisons and generalizations
Negative generalizations that ascribe criminality, inferiority, or moral
corruption to an entire protected group ("all X are ...") are Hateful, even
when phrased as opinion.

## Offensive content (label: Offensive)

### OF-1: Profanity and vulgar language without a protected-group target
Profanity, crude sexual references, or vulgar insults that are NOT directed
at a protected characteristic are Offensive, not Hateful. Example: generic
cursing, scatological humor, crude exclamations.

### OF-2: Insults and abuse directed at individuals
Personal attacks, name-calling, and demeaning language aimed at an individual
(not because of a protected characteristic) are Offensive. Example: calling a
specific person an idiot or worthless.

### OF-3: Reclaimed or in-group usage of slur terms
Uses of slur-derived terms in clearly reclaimed, in-group, or self-referential
contexts, without demeaning intent toward the protected group, are Offensive
rather than Hateful. Context is decisive; when in-group usage cannot be
established, HL-2 applies.

### OF-4: Aggressive or hostile tone without group-based hatred
Hostile, mocking, or belligerent messages that create an unpleasant
environment but do not attack a protected characteristic are Offensive.

## Allowed content (label: Clean)

### CL-1: Ordinary speech
Content that contains no attacks, no profanity directed at people, and no
group-based hostility is Clean, including strong disagreement, criticism of
ideas, and negative product or service reviews.

### CL-2: Counter-speech and quoted hate for condemnation
Quoting, referencing, or describing hateful statements in order to condemn,
refute, report on, or educate about them is Clean. The quoted fragment does
not make the surrounding message Hateful. Example: "I have no respect for
people who write things like '[SLUR] don't belong here'."

### CL-3: Neutral or positive references to protected groups
Neutral mentions of, or positive statements about, protected groups are
Clean, including discussions of identity, culture, and discrimination.

### CL-4: Negated hate
Statements that explicitly negate or reject a hateful proposition ("no group
of people deserves to be treated like that") are Clean, even when they
mention the hateful proposition being rejected.

### CL-5: Homonyms and benign senses of slur-like terms
Words that are spelled or sound like slurs but are used in an unrelated,
benign sense (homonyms, technical terms, place or personal names, ordinary
dictionary senses) are Clean. The surface form alone is not a violation;
HL-2 applies only when the term is used in its slur sense against an
identifiable protected target.
